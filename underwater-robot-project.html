Underwater Biomimetic Robot

A specialized tail mechanism for realistic underwater motion

Project Overview

I led the development of this Underwater Biomimetic Robot for a Turkish defense company while at Cyprus Robotics. The design mimics dolphin-like swimming patterns, focusing on a specialized tail mechanism that overcomes traditional propeller limitations in efficiency, noise, and maneuverability.

The prototype was successfully showcased at the SAHA EXPO International Defence & Aerospace Exhibition in Istanbul.

Key Innovation Points

The most challenging aspects of this project were developing an efficient actuation system that could mimic the complex motion patterns of marine animals while maintaining durability in a saltwater environment. I implemented several innovative solutions:

  • Custom-designed flexible spine mechanism with variable stiffness sections
  • Waterproof servo motors with specialized corrosion-resistant coatings
  • Biomimetic skin material that reduces drag while maintaining flexibility
  • Compact control electronics with advanced motion algorithms
